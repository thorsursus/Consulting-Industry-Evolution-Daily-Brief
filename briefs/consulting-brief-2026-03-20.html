Top Insights

1AI Won't Kill Consulting — But It's Rewriting the RulesAIOpportunity

Capgemini's strategy chief told Fortune (Mar 17) that boards still prefer human advisors for strategic decisions. AI-using consultants complete tasks 25% faster at 40%+ higher quality. Early GenAI adopters see $3.70–$10.30 return per $1 invested.

2Nordic Consultancies Downsizing Despite Market GrowthRiskNordics

Large listed Nordic consultancies cutting headcount to maintain profitability. IT consultants face toughest conditions with surplus of system developer roles.

3Enterprise Architecture Becomes the AI Governance FunctionEAOpportunity

EA in 2026 is defined by AI-first architecture, agentic AI governance, and decision services. Integration has moved from backstage to boardroom-level strategic priority.

4Leadership Needs AI FluencyLeadership

Human+AI leadership models emerging. Leaders must question AI outputs, identify bias, and blend machine efficiency with human judgment.

5Specialization and Outcome-Based Models AccelerateBusiness Model

Clients demand specialists over generalists. Fractional C-suite engagements growing. Outcome-based pricing replacing billable hours.
